<p><p>The HVAC repair, maintenance, and installation market can be categorized into centralized and distributed systems. Centralized HVAC maintenance involves managing a singular, large unit that services an entire building or facility, focusing on efficiency and uniform climate control. In contrast, distributed HVAC maintenance pertains to multiple smaller units dispersed throughout a facility, allowing for localized temperature control. Each type has its own advantages in terms of installation complexity, energy efficiency, and ease of maintenance, catering to different client needs and building designs.</p></p>
