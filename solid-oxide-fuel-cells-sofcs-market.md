<p><p>Solid Oxide Fuel Cells (SOFCs) are electrochemical devices converting chemical energy from fuels into electricity, offering high efficiency and low emissions. The market is categorized into three types: planar, thin film, and tubular. Planar SOFCs feature flat fuel and electrolyte layers, enabling compact designs. Thin-film SOFCs are known for their lightweight and flexible characteristics, suitable for portable applications. Tubular SOFCs present a robust structure with high thermal stability, often used in large-scale stationary power generation, driving diverse market applications.</p></p>
